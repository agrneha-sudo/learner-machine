Part 1 — Foundation

Positioning & The Founder Persona

Before you post a single piece of content, you need to be clear on exactly who you are on screen — and what separates you from every other "AI teacher" on Instagram.

What Makes You Different

Other AI educators: Learned ChatGPT in 2023. Teaching prompts. No business context.

You: Have been building and running companies. You understand operations, teams, decisions under pressure, revenue cycles, business failure, customer relationships.

When you talk about AI, you're not talking about a cool tool — you're talking about what this changes in the reality of running a company. That's a completely different conversation.

The Thought Leader Equation

Trainer: "Here's how to use ChatGPT. Step 1, Step 2, Step 3."

Thought Leader: "I've been building companies for years. The way humans think about work is fundamentally changing — and most business owners don't see it yet. Let me show you what I'm seeing."

One teaches a tool. The other changes how people see the world. People follow world-changers, not tool teachers.

📌 What You Talk About

Business decisions you've actually made. Mistakes you've made running companies. What AI is changing in your actual workflow. What founders and business owners are getting wrong about AI. The history of how businesses adapted to big technological shifts.

🚫 What You Never Do

Never say "Here's a tutorial." Never say "Step 1, Step 2." Never position yourself as a course seller. Never use language like "I'll teach you." Instead: "I've been thinking about this." "Here's what I'm seeing." "This changed how I run my company."

🎯 Who You're Talking To

Curious but overwhelmed business owners. Founders who feel behind. Smart professionals who sense AI is important but don't know where to start. People who want to understand — not just follow tutorials. They want a guide, not a teacher.

The Golden Rule of Thought Leadership: Never explain what you know. Share what you've lived. Every post should feel like a conversation with someone who has been in the room where decisions are made — not a lesson from someone who read a book about it.

Part 2 — Content Architecture

5 Content Pillars

Every post you create belongs to one of these pillars. Together they build a complete worldview — not just a collection of tips.

🏗️

Pillar 1 — "The Builder's Lens" (25%)

Stories and insights from the actual experience of starting and running companies. The decisions, the failures, the pivots. This is what makes you credible. Everything else is built on this foundation.

Examples: "The day I realised my entire operations team was doing AI's job." | "Why I almost hired 3 people — and then didn't." | "What 10 years of building companies taught me about systems — and why AI is the logical conclusion."

🤖

Pillar 2 — "AI Decoded" (25%)

Explaining AI concepts as a founder would — not technically, but through the lens of what it means for business and society. Starting with curiosity, building to depth. This is where generative AI vs other AI lives.

Examples: "AI didn't start with ChatGPT — here's the 70-year story nobody tells." | "Generative AI vs the AI you've been using for years — what's actually different." | "Why most people confuse AI with automation — and why that confusion is expensive."

⚡

Pillar 3 — "AI in My Company" (20%)

Real, honest, specific account of how you're applying AI to your own business right now. Not polished success stories — actual experiments, surprises, failures, and wins. This is the most shareable content because it's real.

Examples: "I automated our entire customer onboarding last week. Here's what happened." | "AI wrote our marketing plan. I hated 40% of it — and that 40% was the most interesting part." | "3 tasks I completely removed from my to-do list by giving them to AI."

🔭

Pillar 4 — "The Future I'm Seeing" (20%)

Contrarian, forward-looking takes. What's coming that most people aren't ready for. This is pure thought leadership — opinions backed by experience and pattern recognition. People follow you because you see around corners.

Examples: "The job title 'Manager' will look completely different in 5 years." | "Most startups in 2025 are solving yesterday's problem." | "The companies that will win the next decade aren't the ones with the best AI — they're the ones with the best questions."

🌱

Pillar 5 — "For the Curious Beginner" (10%)

Accessible entry points for people completely new to AI. But even here — framed through the founder's lens. Not "here's a tutorial" but "here's what I wish I understood about this when I first started." Always bridges to the bigger business picture.

Examples: "If you've never used ChatGPT — start here. A founder's first steps." | "I explained AI to my 60-year-old father last week. Here's what I said." | "The simplest mental model for understanding AI — one that actually holds up."

Part 3 — Content Arc

Your content should build on itself like chapters in a book. Week 1 plants curiosity. Week 2 adds depth. Week 3 connects AI to business reality. Week 4 positions you as the person people need to follow.

The single most important clarification in the AI conversation. Explained through business examples. This carousel will be saved and shared heavily by professionals.

Slide 1 — Cover
"Everyone talks about AI. Almost nobody knows there are 3 completely different things being called AI."

Dark. Authoritative headline. "Swipe for the map." — founder's name.

Slide 2
Type 1: Automation AI ⚙️

What it does: Follows rules. If X happens → do Y. Zero learning.

Examples: Email auto-replies, payment reminders, inventory alerts, spam filters.

Think of it as: A very reliable employee who follows a manual perfectly — but can't handle anything outside the manual.

Slide 3
Type 2: Machine Learning AI 📊

What it does: Learns from data. Finds patterns. Makes predictions. Gets better over time.

Examples: Netflix recommendations, Google Ads targeting, credit score calculations, fraud detection.

Think of it as: An analyst who has read every customer file and finds patterns no human could see in that volume of data.

Slide 4
Type 3: Generative AI ✨ ← This is the new one

What it does: Creates new content. Writes, designs, codes, reasons, plans — from a simple text instruction.

Examples: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Midjourney, DALL-E.

Think of it as: A junior employee with unlimited reading time who can draft anything, explain anything, and never gets tired — waiting for your direction.

Slide 5
The Critical Difference 🎯

Automation AI: Does what you programmed. Rigid.

Machine Learning: Predicts based on past data. Analytical.

Generative AI: Creates something that didn't exist before. Based on your request. Right now. This is new.

Slide 6
Real Business Scenario 🏢

Automation AI: Sends invoice reminder at day 30

Machine Learning: Predicts which clients are likely to be late payers

Generative AI: Writes a personalised, empathetic follow-up email for that specific client based on your relationship history

All three. Different jobs. Different value.

Slide 7
The Founder's Framework 🧭

Ask about every business task:

→ Repetitive rule? Automate it.
→ Pattern in data? ML it.
→ Needs thinking/writing/creativity? Generative AI it.
→ Needs real human judgment? Keep a human.
