Business Idea Validation

Test, refine, and validate your business ideas before investing time or money. Aage badhne se pehle, idea solid hai ya nahi — pata karo.

Prompt #1 The Problem-Solution Stress Test
"I have a business idea: [DESCRIBE YOUR IDEA IN 2-3 SENTENCES]. Act as a skeptical angel investor from India who has seen hundreds of pitches. List the top 7 reasons this idea might fail, then for each reason suggest one concrete way I can fix or de-risk it. Be brutally honest — don't sugarcoat."
💡 Use when: You have a raw idea and want to stress-test it before sharing with anyone.
Prompt #2 Ideal Customer Profile Builder
"My business idea is [YOUR IDEA]. Help me build a detailed Ideal Customer Profile (ICP). Include: age range, income level (in Indian Rupees), location (tier-1/tier-2/tier-3 city), job or life situation, daily frustrations, what they currently do to solve the problem, and where they spend time online. Make it hyper-specific, not generic."
💡 Use when: You need to define exactly WHO you are solving the problem for.
Prompt #3 Validation Interview Script Generator
"I want to validate my business idea: [YOUR IDEA]. Write me a 10-question customer discovery interview script I can use to talk to potential customers. Questions should uncover: whether the problem is real, how painful it is, what they've tried before, and how much they'd pay for a solution. Include follow-up probes for each question. Format it so I can print and use it in a 20-minute call."
💡 Use when: You're about to do customer interviews and need a structured script.
Prompt #4 Market Size Estimator (Indian Context)
"Help me estimate the market size for [YOUR BUSINESS IDEA] in India. Use the TAM-SAM-SOM framework. Make reasonable assumptions based on India's population, internet penetration, smartphone users, and average spending patterns in the [INDUSTRY] sector. Show your math step by step and tell me if this market is worth pursuing for a bootstrapped Indian startup."
💡 Use when: You need to know if your market is large enough to build a real business.
Prompt #5 Unfair Advantage Identifier
"Here is my business idea: [YOUR IDEA]. Here is my background: [YOUR SKILLS, EXPERIENCE, CONNECTIONS, RESOURCES]. Analyze what unfair advantages I have that most people starting this business wouldn't have. Then tell me which advantages are truly defensible vs. which ones competitors can easily copy. Help me figure out what to double down on."
💡 Use when: You want to understand why YOU are the right person to build this business.
Prompt #6 Pre-Mortem Analysis
"It is 2 years from today and my business [YOUR IDEA] has failed completely. Write a detailed post-mortem report explaining exactly what went wrong. Cover: market timing mistakes, customer acquisition failures, product problems, team issues, financial mistakes, and competitive threats specific to the Indian market. Then suggest what I should do in the next 90 days to prevent each failure mode."
💡 Use when: You want to think through failure scenarios before they happen.
Prompt #7 Competitor Landscape Scan
"My business idea is [YOUR IDEA] targeting [TARGET AUDIENCE] in India. List all the direct and indirect competitors — including Indian startups, established Indian companies, and global players operating in India. For each, estimate their strengths and weaknesses from a customer's perspective. Then identify the gap in the market that none of them are filling well."
💡 Use when: You need a quick competitive landscape overview before diving deep.
Prompt #8 Revenue Model Generator
"I have a business idea: [YOUR IDEA]. Generate 8 different revenue models I could use to monetize this, ranging from one-time sales to subscriptions to marketplace commissions. For each model, estimate: realistic pricing in INR, how many customers I'd need to reach ₹1 lakh/month, and the biggest challenge with that model in the Indian market where price sensitivity is high."
💡 Use when: You know what you're building but not how to make money from it.
Prompt #9 Landing Page Headline Test
"My business idea is [YOUR IDEA] for [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Write 10 different landing page headlines I could use to test which positioning resonates most. Make each headline use a different angle: pain-focused, outcome-focused, curiosity-driven, social proof, speed/ease, price/value, fear of missing out, credibility, comparison to alternatives, and emotion-driven. Keep each under 12 words."
💡 Use when: You want to validate messaging before building a full product.
Prompt #10 Minimum Viable Product Definer
"I want to build [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE IDEA]. Help me define the absolute minimum viable version I can launch in 30 days with less than ₹50,000. List only the features that are absolutely necessary to deliver the core value and get the first 10 paying customers. Then list 5 features I am tempted to build but should NOT build yet, and explain why."
💡 Use when: You're overcomplicating your first version and need to simplify.
Prompt #11 Pricing Sensitivity Analyzer
"My product/service is [DESCRIPTION] for [TARGET CUSTOMER] in India. Help me think through pricing strategy using the Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Model. Ask me the four key questions, then based on my answers, suggest an optimal price point. Also tell me what price signals 'cheap/low quality' and what price signals 'too expensive' for this specific Indian audience."
💡 Use when: You're unsure what to charge and need a framework to decide.
Prompt #12 Assumption Mapping Exercise
"My business idea is [YOUR IDEA]. List every major assumption my business model depends on — about customers, the problem, the solution, the market, distribution, pricing, and competition. Then rank each assumption from highest to lowest risk based on: (1) how important it is to my success and (2) how uncertain it currently is. Tell me which 3 assumptions I must validate first."
💡 Use when: You want to systematically de-risk your idea with structured validation.
Prompt #13 Go-to-Market Channel Selector
"I am launching [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE] for [TARGET AUDIENCE] in India. My budget for the first 3 months is ₹[AMOUNT]. Recommend the top 3 customer acquisition channels I should test first, ranked by expected cost per customer and speed of feedback. For each channel, give me a specific 2-week experiment I can run to test if it works before committing more budget."
💡 Use when: You're deciding where to spend your early marketing budget.
Prompt #14 Indian Market Localization Check
"I am adapting [PRODUCT/SERVICE — could be a foreign model or your own idea] for the Indian market. Identify 10 specific ways the Indian market is different from Western markets that will affect my business model. Cover: payment preferences (UPI/cash), language diversity, trust factors, price sensitivity, family decision-making dynamics, regional differences, and regulatory considerations. Suggest how I adapt for each."
💡 Use when: You're launching in India and need to localize beyond just translating content.
Prompt #15 10 Customer Objections Simulator
"My product is [PRODUCT DESCRIPTION] priced at ₹[PRICE]. Roleplay as 5 different types of Indian customers (conservative housewife, cost-conscious small business owner, skeptical college student, busy professional, and first-generation entrepreneur from a small city). For each persona, give me their top 2 objections to buying my product and the exact response I should use to handle each objection."
💡 Use when: You need to prepare for sales conversations and objection handling.
Prompt #16 Pivoting Brainstorm
"My original business idea was [ORIGINAL IDEA] but it's not getting traction because [REASON — e.g., no one is paying, wrong audience, too much competition]. Based on what I've learned, suggest 6 pivot options. For each pivot, explain: what changes vs. what stays the same, what new customer problem it solves, and what's the fastest way to test if this pivot is worth pursuing."
💡 Use when: Your idea isn't working and you need to pivot intelligently.
Prompt #17 Unit Economics Calculator
"Help me calculate unit economics for my business: [DESCRIBE YOUR BUSINESS MODEL]. My product/service sells for ₹[PRICE]. My variable costs per sale are approximately ₹[COST]. Calculate: Gross Margin, Customer Acquisition Cost target to stay profitable, Lifetime Value needed for a healthy LTV:CAC ratio, and the monthly revenue target to break even if my fixed costs are ₹[MONTHLY FIXED COSTS]. Show all calculations clearly."
💡 Use when: You need to know if your business model is fundamentally profitable.
Prompt #18 Fake Door Test Designer
"I want to validate demand for [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE] before building it. Design a 'fake door' test I can run in 7 days using just a simple landing page and social media. Include: the exact headline and CTA to use, what I offer visitors in exchange for their email or pre-order, how I measure success (what conversion rate means I should proceed), and how to handle people who try to buy before the product exists."
💡 Use when: You want real demand signal (not just surveys) without building anything first.
Prompt #19 Regulatory & Compliance Checklist
"I want to start a [TYPE OF BUSINESS] in India in the [INDUSTRY] sector. Give me a comprehensive checklist of all legal, regulatory, and compliance requirements I need to be aware of. Cover: business registration (Sole Proprietor vs. LLP vs. Pvt Ltd), GST registration, industry-specific licenses, FSSAI/MSME/other certifications if applicable, and any digital/e-commerce regulations. Flag which ones I must do on Day 1 vs. which can wait."
💡 Use when: You're starting a new business and need to understand legal requirements.
Prompt #20 90-Day Validation Roadmap
"My business idea is [YOUR IDEA]. I have ₹[BUDGET] and [NUMBER] hours per week to work on this. Create a detailed 90-day validation roadmap broken into three 30-day phases. Each phase should have: specific goals, key actions, success metrics, and a decision checkpoint — telling me what results mean I should continue, pivot, or stop. Make it realistic for someone working on this as a side project alongside a job."
💡 Use when: You need a structured plan to validate your idea without quitting your job prematurely.

Market Research & Competitor Analysis

Understand your market, your customers, and your competition before competitors understand you. Data-driven decisions se hi business banta hai.

Prompt #21 Customer Pain Point Deep Dive
"I am researching the [INDUSTRY/NICHE] market in India targeting [TARGET AUDIENCE]. List the top 10 most painful, frustrating problems this audience faces related to [PROBLEM AREA]. For each problem, rate it on a scale of 1-10 for: frequency (how often they face it), intensity (how painful it is), and budget (how much they'd pay to solve it). Prioritize the problems with the highest combined score."
💡 Use when: You want to identify which customer problem is worth building a business around.
Prompt #22 Competitor SWOT Matrix
"Analyze my top competitor: [COMPETITOR NAME/DESCRIPTION]. Build a detailed SWOT analysis from the perspective of their customers, not their investors. Under Strengths: what do customers genuinely love? Under Weaknesses: what do customers complain about in reviews? Under Opportunities: what customer needs are they not serving well? Under Threats: what would make customers switch away from them? Use this to find my entry point."
💡 Use when: You want to find gaps in a competitor's offering you can exploit.
Prompt #23 Customer Review Mining Template
"Here are 10 customer reviews of [COMPETITOR PRODUCT/SERVICE]: [PASTE REVIEWS]. Analyze these reviews and extract: (1) the top 5 things customers love most, (2) the top 5 complaints or frustrations, (3) exact words and phrases customers use to describe their problem (these are gold for my marketing copy), and (4) unmet needs — things customers wish existed but don't. Give me a 'voice of customer' summary I can use directly."
💡 Use when: You have competitor reviews and want to extract strategic insights fast.
Prompt #24 Trend Spotter for Indian Market
"I run a business in the [INDUSTRY] space in India. Identify 8 emerging trends in this industry that are likely to become mainstream in India over the next 2-3 years. For each trend, tell me: what's driving it (technology, demographics, policy, culture), which Indian cities or segments are adopting it first, and how an entrepreneur can position their business to ride this trend now before it peaks."
💡 Use when: You want to get ahead of trends and position your business for future growth.
Prompt #25 Persona Builder with Buying Journey
"Create a detailed buyer persona for [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Name them, give them a specific job, location, income, and family situation relevant to India. Then map their complete buying journey: (1) what triggers them to start looking for a solution, (2) where they search for information (Google, YouTube, Instagram, WhatsApp groups), (3) what they compare before deciding, (4) what finally makes them buy, and (5) what makes them refer others."
💡 Use when: You need to understand the full path a customer takes before buying from you.
Prompt #26 Survey Question Designer
"I want to survey [TARGET AUDIENCE] about [TOPIC/PROBLEM] to inform my business decisions. Write a 12-question survey that will give me actionable data. Mix question types: multiple choice, rating scales (1-5), and 2-3 open-ended questions. Include a screener question at the start to filter out irrelevant respondents. Make the language conversational and easy to understand for a non-English-first audience in India."
💡 Use when: You need primary research data from your target market.
Prompt #27 Competitor Pricing Reverse-Engineer
"My competitor [COMPETITOR NAME] sells [THEIR PRODUCT/SERVICE] at [THEIR PRICE]. Help me reverse-engineer their pricing strategy. What does their price signal about their positioning? What customer segment are they targeting with this price? Where does this leave room for me to compete — should I price higher (premium), lower (value), or differently (different model like subscription vs. one-time)? What would each pricing approach mean for my brand perception?"
💡 Use when: You're trying to figure out how to price relative to an existing competitor.
Prompt #28 Job-to-be-Done Framework Analyzer
"Apply the Jobs-to-be-Done framework to [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE]. For my target customer [TARGET AUDIENCE], identify: (1) the functional job they're hiring my product to do, (2) the emotional job (how do they want to feel?), and (3) the social job (how do they want to be perceived by others?). Then identify the 'job executor' — the moment that triggers them to look for a solution. Use this to sharpen my messaging."
💡 Use when: Your marketing isn't resonating and you need to understand the deeper motivation behind purchases.
Prompt #29 Market Segmentation Matrix
"I am in the [INDUSTRY] business in India. Help me segment the market using 3 different dimensions: demographic (age, income, location), psychographic (values, lifestyle, attitude), and behavioral (usage frequency, price sensitivity, brand loyalty). For each meaningful segment you identify, estimate its size (small/medium/large), willingness to pay, and ease to reach. Recommend which segment I should target first and why."
💡 Use when: Your market feels too broad and you need to pick your best initial beachhead.
Prompt #30 Indirect Competitor Mapper
"My direct product is [YOUR PRODUCT] but I want to understand my full competitive landscape. What are ALL the ways my target customer [TARGET AUDIENCE] is currently solving the problem of [PROBLEM] — including: doing nothing, DIY workarounds, paying a person instead of a product, using a completely different category of product, or accepting the status quo? Map each alternative with its cost to the customer (in money AND time AND frustration)."
💡 Use when: You want to understand who you're really competing against, not just obvious competitors.
Prompt #31 Niche Down Advisor
"I want to start a [TYPE OF BUSINESS] in India. Currently my target audience is too broad: [YOUR CURRENT TARGET AUDIENCE]. Help me niche down by identifying 6 specific sub-niches within this market. For each sub-niche, tell me: what specific problem they have, why they're underserved, what they'd pay, and how easy they are to reach. Recommend the one niche that gives me the best chance of becoming the clear market leader within 12 months."
💡 Use when: You're trying to sell to everyone and converting no one.
Prompt #32 Keyword & Search Intent Research
"I run a [BUSINESS TYPE] in India targeting [AUDIENCE]. List 30 keywords my potential customers would search for on Google when they have the problem I solve. Group them by search intent: (1) informational (learning about the problem), (2) navigational (looking for a specific brand), and (3) transactional (ready to buy). Mark which keywords are likely high-competition vs. low-competition opportunities for a new business."
💡 Use when: You're planning SEO or content strategy and need to understand what your customers are searching for.
Prompt #33 Feature Comparison Matrix Creator
"Create a feature comparison matrix between my product [YOUR PRODUCT] and these competitors: [LIST 3-4 COMPETITORS]. The features to compare are: [LIST 8-10 KEY FEATURES]. Mark each cell as: ✓ (has it), ✗ (doesn't have it), or ~ (partial). Then identify: where I win, where I lose, and where I have a unique feature no competitor has. Use this to write my positioning statement and key differentiators."
💡 Use when: You need to clearly articulate why your product is different and better.
Prompt #34 Social Listening Report Generator
"I am researching what [TARGET AUDIENCE] in India are saying online about [TOPIC/PROBLEM/COMPETITOR]. Here is a sample of posts/comments I found: [PASTE 5-10 SOCIAL MEDIA POSTS OR COMMENTS]. Analyze the sentiment, identify recurring complaints, extract the emotional language people use, spot any unmet needs or wishlist items, and tell me what content or product angle would get the most engagement from this community."
💡 Use when: You want to turn social media listening into strategic insights.
Prompt #35 Industry Report Summarizer
"Here is a section from an industry report about [INDUSTRY] in India: [PASTE REPORT SECTION]. Summarize the key findings in plain language. Then tell me: (1) what opportunity this data points to for a small business or startup, (2) what risk or challenge it highlights, and (3) what action I should take in the next 30 days based on this data. Translate jargon into simple language a first-generation entrepreneur would understand."
💡 Use when: You have market data but need help turning it into business decisions.
Prompt #36 Price Elasticity Tester Script
"I want to test how price-sensitive my customers are for [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Write me a script for a 5-minute phone conversation where I can naturally test willingness to pay without it feeling like a sales call. Include: how to open the conversation, how to describe the product, how to anchor a price and gauge reaction, and how to test a higher price without losing the prospect. Tailor this for Indian customers who often negotiate and compare prices."
💡 Use when: You need to test pricing in real conversations before publishing a price.
Prompt #37 Whitespace Opportunity Finder
"In the [INDUSTRY] market in India, the main players are [LIST COMPETITORS]. Analyze the competitive landscape and identify 5 'whitespace' opportunities — segments, use cases, geographies, price points, or customer types that no one is serving well. For each whitespace, estimate: market size, difficulty to capture, and what minimum viable offering would let me test this opportunity within 60 days and under ₹1 lakh."
💡 Use when: You want to find blue ocean opportunities in a crowded market.
Prompt #38 Distribution Channel Analyzer
"I sell [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE] in India. Map out all the possible distribution channels — both online (D2C website, Amazon/Flipkart, Instagram shop, WhatsApp commerce, Meesho, etc.) and offline (retail distributors, direct sales team, franchise, channel partners). For each channel that makes sense for my business, estimate: setup cost, time to first sale, profit margin impact, and how much control I retain over the customer relationship."
💡 Use when: You need to decide how to get your product in front of customers at scale.
Prompt #39 NPS & Customer Feedback Analyzer
"Here are NPS responses and feedback comments from my customers: [PASTE FEEDBACK]. Categorize the feedback into themes. For Promoters (score 9-10): what specific things are driving their love? For Passives (7-8): what would move them to promoter? For Detractors (0-6): what is the root cause of their dissatisfaction and what would it take to win them back? Create a prioritized action list of product and service improvements."
💡 Use when: You have customer feedback and need to turn it into a product improvement roadmap.
Prompt #40 Annual Market Research Plan
"I run a [BUSINESS TYPE] in India with a team of [NUMBER] people and a quarterly research budget of ₹[AMOUNT]. Create a 12-month market research calendar. Include: what to research each quarter, which research methods to use (customer interviews, surveys, competitor audits, social listening, sales data analysis), who on my team should own each activity, what deliverable each research produces, and how it feeds into business decisions. Keep it practical and executable."
💡 Use when: You want to make market research a regular business habit, not a one-time activity.

Brand Building & Positioning

Build a brand that customers remember, trust, and recommend. Ek strong brand hi aapka sabse bada competitive advantage hai.

Prompt #41 Brand Name Generator
"I need a brand name for my [TYPE OF BUSINESS] that serves [TARGET AUDIENCE] in India. Generate 20 brand name options across these styles: (1) descriptive names that explain what I do, (2) invented/made-up words that are easy to pronounce in Hindi and English, (3) metaphor-based names that evoke the feeling I want to create, and (4) founder-story-based names. For each, check if it's easy to spell, say in Hindi, and likely available as a .com domain."
💡 Use when: You're starting from scratch and need naming options to evaluate.
Prompt #42 Brand Positioning Statement Writer
"Help me write a brand positioning statement for [YOUR BRAND/PRODUCT]. Use this format: 'For [TARGET AUDIENCE] who [NEED OR PROBLEM], [BRAND NAME] is the [CATEGORY] that [KEY BENEFIT] because [REASON TO BELIEVE].' Generate 5 versions with different angles — each emphasizing a different benefit. Then help me evaluate which one is most defensible, differentiating, and emotionally resonant for Indian customers."
💡 Use when: You need a clear positioning statement to guide all your marketing decisions.
Prompt #43 Brand Voice & Tone Guide
"I am building a brand called [BRAND NAME] for [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Create a Brand Voice Guide. Define: (1) 4 brand personality adjectives with an explanation of what each means in practice, (2) 3 things our brand always says/stands for, (3) 3 things our brand never says/does, (4) the tone in different contexts (social media vs. customer service vs. sales vs. blog), and (5) 5 before/after examples showing our voice vs. a generic corporate voice."
💡 Use when: You need to ensure consistent tone across all your content and communications.
Prompt #44 Tagline Workshop
"Create 15 tagline options for [YOUR BRAND], which offers [WHAT YOU DO] to [TARGET AUDIENCE]. The taglines should: be under 6 words, be memorable when heard aloud, work in both an English-first and Hindi-first audience, and capture the core emotional promise of the brand. Group them by approach: benefit-focused, emotion-focused, challenge-focused, and aspirational. Highlight your top 3 picks and explain why."
💡 Use when: You need a short, powerful tagline for your brand.
Prompt #45 Origin Story Crafting
"Help me write my brand's origin story. Here are the facts: [TELL YOUR STORY IN BULLET POINTS — why you started, what problem you faced, what turning point happened, what you built]. Transform these facts into a compelling narrative using the classic hero's journey structure. It should be authentic, relatable for Indian entrepreneurs, 250-300 words long, and end with what drives us forward today. This will be used on our About page and in investor pitches."
💡 Use when: You need to tell your founding story in a way that connects emotionally with customers.
Prompt #46 Brand Archetype Identifier
"Based on the following brand description: [YOUR BRAND, WHAT IT DOES, WHO IT SERVES, ITS VALUES], identify which of the 12 brand archetypes fits best (Hero, Sage, Rebel, Magician, Explorer, Creator, Ruler, Caregiver, Everyman, Jester, Lover, Innocent). Then tell me: what messaging themes align with this archetype, what visual and verbal cues should the brand use, and which Indian brands successfully embody this archetype that I can study."
💡 Use when: You want to build a brand with consistent personality across all touchpoints.
Prompt #47 Value Proposition Canvas
"Fill out a Value Proposition Canvas for [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Customer Profile side: list their jobs-to-be-done, pains, and gains for [TARGET CUSTOMER]. Value Map side: list your pain relievers, gain creators, and products/services. Then identify the 'fit' — where your offering most directly addresses their most important pains and gains. Show me where I have strong fit and where I have gaps I need to address."
💡 Use when: You need to ensure your product genuinely solves what matters most to customers.
Prompt #48 Competitor Differentiation Statement
"My competitors are [LIST 2-3 MAIN COMPETITORS]. My product/service is [YOUR OFFERING]. Write 5 differentiation statements that clearly explain why a customer should choose me over each competitor. Each statement should follow this format: 'Unlike [COMPETITOR], we [KEY DIFFERENCE] so you can [CUSTOMER BENEFIT].' Make them specific — not vague claims like 'better quality' but concrete, provable differences."
💡 Use when: Customers ask 'why should I choose you?' and you need a crisp answer.
Prompt #49 Brand Color & Visual Identity Brief
"I need to create a visual identity for my brand [BRAND NAME] in the [INDUSTRY] space targeting [AUDIENCE]. Write a visual identity brief for a designer that includes: brand personality (5 adjectives), the emotion the brand should make people feel, color palette recommendation with reasoning (and how it compares to competitors' colors), typography style direction (serif/sans-serif, modern/classic), and 5 reference brands whose visual identity I should draw inspiration from."
💡 Use when: You're briefing a designer or building your visual identity direction.
Prompt #50 Brand Promise & Guarantee Writer
"Write a brand promise and guarantee for [YOUR BUSINESS]. The promise should be: specific (not vague), customer-outcome-focused (not feature-focused), memorable in one sentence, and something we can actually deliver. Then create a risk-reversal guarantee that removes the customer's fear of buying. Make the guarantee bold enough to differentiate us but realistic enough to actually honor. Consider Indian customer psychology where trust is earned slowly."
💡 Use when: You want to reduce buyer hesitation and build trust through a strong guarantee.
Prompt #51 Social Media Bio Generator
"Write social media bios for [YOUR BRAND/PERSONAL BRAND] for these platforms: Instagram (150 chars max), LinkedIn (220 chars), Twitter/X (160 chars), and YouTube (channel description 500 chars). For each, tailor the tone to the platform's culture. Include: what I do, who I help, why I'm different, and a call to action. Also write a WhatsApp Business description (139 chars). My brand is [DESCRIBE YOUR BRAND AND WHAT MAKES IT UNIQUE]."
💡 Use when: You're setting up or refreshing your social media presence.
Prompt #52 Personal Brand LinkedIn Headline
"I am an entrepreneur in [INDUSTRY] who [WHAT YOU DO/HAVE ACHIEVED]. Write 10 LinkedIn headline options that position me as a thought leader and attract [TARGET AUDIENCE — clients/investors/partners]. Avoid generic titles like 'Founder & CEO'. Instead, lead with outcomes, expertise, or mission. Include a mix of: result-focused (I help X do Y), credibility-focused (keywords + achievement), and conversation-starting (curiosity-driven) headlines."
💡 Use when: You want your LinkedIn profile to attract the right opportunities.
Prompt #53 Brand Story for Pitch Deck
"Write the 'Why We Exist' slide content for my pitch deck. My startup is [STARTUP NAME], we solve [PROBLEM] for [AUDIENCE]. Here's our origin: [YOUR STORY IN 3-4 SENTENCES]. Transform this into: (1) a one-sentence mission statement, (2) a 3-sentence brand story for the slide, and (3) a 60-second verbal script I can deliver when presenting this slide to investors. Make it emotionally compelling while staying factually grounded."
💡 Use when: You're preparing an investor pitch and need to nail the emotional 'why'.
Prompt #54 Brand Consistency Auditor
"Audit my brand consistency across these touchpoints. Here are examples of my current communications: Website homepage: [PASTE TEXT]. Instagram bio: [PASTE]. A recent email I sent: [PASTE]. A product description: [PASTE]. Identify: inconsistencies in tone, messaging gaps where we don't communicate our value clearly, opportunities to strengthen brand personality, and 5 specific improvements to make my brand feel cohesive and professional."
💡 Use when: Your brand feels scattered and you want to create a more consistent identity.
Prompt #55 Trust Signal Builder
"I run [YOUR BUSINESS] and my target customers are [AUDIENCE]. List 15 specific trust signals I should add to my website, social media, and sales conversations to increase customer confidence in buying from me. Prioritize signals that work specifically for Indian customers who are skeptical of online businesses. Include: social proof types, security indicators, transparency moves, third-party validations, and community signals. For each, give a quick implementation tip."
💡 Use when: Potential customers are hesitant to buy because they don't trust you enough yet.
Prompt #56 Rebranding Strategy Planner
"I need to rebrand my business [CURRENT BRAND NAME] because [REASON — e.g., name confusion, wrong positioning, scaling to new market]. Create a rebranding strategy that covers: (1) what to keep vs. what to change, (2) a phased rollout plan to avoid confusing existing customers, (3) how to communicate the rebrand to customers, employees, and social media followers, and (4) what metrics to track to know if the rebrand is working. Timeline: 60-90 days."
