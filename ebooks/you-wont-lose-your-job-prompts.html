The Skill Gap Auditor

Find out exactly what you're missing — before a recruiter does.
Why This Prompt Matters

Most students spend years studying the wrong things and only find out in the interview room. This prompt builds a personalised skill gap report in 60 seconds — comparing where you are today against what your target role actually needs.

The output tells you what to learn, what to stop wasting time on, and which of your existing skills to lead with. It's the career counsellor session you never got. 

I'm a [your degree / field] student targeting a role as [job title] at [type of company, e.g. a mid-size startup / an MNC / a government PSU].

My current skills include: [list 5–8 skills you have].

Do the following:
1. List the top 10 skills typically required for this role in 2024–2025
2. Mark each as: ✅ I have it | ⚠️ I partially have it | ❌ I'm missing it
3. For every ❌ or ⚠️, suggest the single fastest way to build that skill (free resource preferred)
4. Rank my overall readiness out of 10 and give me one honest paragraph about where I stand

Be direct. Don't sugarcoat. I want the truth.
What You Get

A personalised skill audit with a readiness score and a prioritised learning list — in 2 minutes.

Use It When

Starting a job search, returning from a break, or unsure what to add to your resume next.

Pro Tip

After the first output, follow up with: "Now create a 30-day study plan to close my top 3 skill gaps, with daily tasks I can do in under 1 hour." You'll get a full learning roadmap instantly.

Try These Variations
Replace "mid-size startup" with "Big 4 consulting firm" or "government bank" for totally different output
Run this prompt again after 30 days of upskilling to track your progress
Use it for internships too — just swap "job title" with "internship role"

The AI-Proof Resume Builder

Make your resume scream "human edge" — not just qualifications.
Why This Prompt Matters

Recruiters are being flooded with AI-generated resumes that all sound identical. This prompt does the opposite — it rewrites your bullet points to highlight the human skills that AI cannot replace: leadership, judgment, creativity, and collaboration.

It also optimises for ATS (applicant tracking systems) so your resume gets read by a human in the first place. 

Here is my current resume (or a description of my experience):
[paste your resume text OR describe your top 3–5 experiences in plain language]

I'm applying for: [job title] at [type/name of company]

Rewrite my resume bullet points to:
1. Lead with impact and outcomes, not tasks (use numbers wherever possible)
2. Highlight human-only skills: leadership, judgment, creativity, cross-team work
3. Remove or downplay anything that sounds like it could be automated
4. Add 2–3 new bullet points I may have forgotten to include, based on common experiences in my field
5. Suggest a 2-line summary that positions me as someone who leads AI, not fears it

Output as ready-to-paste bullet points.
What You Get

A fully rewritten, human-first resume that stands out in a sea of AI-generated applications.

Use It When

Applying for jobs, updating LinkedIn, or whenever your resume hasn't been refreshed in 6+ months.

Pro Tip

After rewriting, ask: "Now write a 3-sentence cover letter opening that references a specific challenge in this industry and positions me as someone who solves it using AI." Instant differentiation.

Try These Variations
Ask it to tailor the same resume for 3 different job descriptions back to back
Use for LinkedIn "About" section rewrites — same prompt, different output
Ask it to identify which bullet points a recruiter will skip — then fix only those

The Career Roadmap Generator

Build a 90-day plan toward a role AI won't make obsolete.
Why This Prompt Matters

The biggest career mistake students make is chasing the same roles as everyone else — roles that are shrinking. This prompt helps you identify AI-resistant career paths and gives you a concrete 90-day sprint to get there.

It factors in your existing degree, interests, and the direction automation is actually heading — not just what was popular 5 years ago. 

I am a [degree + year, e.g. 3rd year B.Com student] with an interest in [1–2 interests or strengths, e.g. data analysis and talking to people].

My realistic goal: land a good job within [timeframe, e.g. 6 months] that won't be automated in the next 10 years.

Do the following:
1. List 5 career paths that match my profile AND are highly resistant to AI automation — explain why each is safe
2. Rank them by: a) ease of entry, b) income potential, c) long-term safety
3. For my top recommended path, build a 90-day action plan with:
   - Week-by-week focus areas
   - 3 specific online resources (free preferred) per month
   - One "proof of work" project to build per month that I can show in interviews
4. Tell me one thing I should STOP doing because it's a waste of time

Give me the honest version, not the encouraging one.
What You Get

A personalised 90-day career roadmap, AI-safe career options ranked, and a project plan you can actually execute.

Use It When

In your final year, after a failed placement attempt, or when you feel stuck and directionless.

Pro Tip

Run this prompt once a quarter and update the timeframe. Career paths shift fast — an outdated roadmap is worse than no roadmap.

Try These Variations
Add "I want to eventually work remotely for international clients" to get globally-relevant paths
Swap "6 months" for "2 years" to get a deeper, more ambitious plan
Ask it to compare two specific paths head-to-head with a pros/cons breakdown

The Daily Work Amplifier

Do 3 hours of work in 45 minutes. Every single day.
Why This Prompt Matters

The professional who finishes at 3 PM what takes their colleague until 7 PM looks like a genius. They're not — they just use AI right. This prompt is a universal "task accelerator" you adapt every morning to your actual workload.

It doesn't just help you go faster — it makes your output look sharper, more structured, and more thoughtful than what most people produce in double the time. 

I need to complete the following tasks today:
[List 3–5 actual tasks you need to do today, e.g. "Write a performance review for my team member", "Prepare talking points for a client call", "Summarise last month's sales data into a slide"]

My role: [your job title and 1-line description of what you do]
My audience for these tasks: [who will see/receive this output — manager, client, team, etc.]

For each task:
1. Give me a ready-to-use first draft OR a bullet-point outline I can expand
2. Highlight the 2–3 things my audience actually cares about most
3. Tell me one thing to add that will make this stand out from average work
4. Estimate how long this should realistically take me with AI assistance

Start with the task that will have the highest impact if done well.
What You Get

Ready-to-use drafts for your highest-impact tasks with strategic tips that make your work look exceptional.

Use It When

Every Monday morning as a weekly kickstart, or any day when your to-do list feels overwhelming.

Pro Tip

Turn this into a 5-minute morning ritual. Paste your calendar into the prompt and ask AI to prioritise your day for maximum impact. Your manager will notice within 2 weeks.

Try These Variations
Add "My boss cares most about: [X]" to get output perfectly calibrated to your manager's priorities
End with "Also flag any task I should delegate or skip entirely" — instant time savings
For emails and reports, ask: "Write this at a Level 8 out of 10 quality, not perfect — I need speed"

The Strategic Thinker Upgrade

Build the kind of insights your manager can't stop talking about.
Why This Prompt Matters

Most professionals execute tasks. The ones who get promoted have opinions, see patterns, and bring strategic perspectives to the table. That skill used to take years to develop. With AI, you can shortcut it — right now.

This prompt takes raw information — a report, a meeting, a situation at work — and transforms it into sharp, structured thinking that sounds senior-level. 

Here is a situation / data / report I'm dealing with at work:
[Describe the situation in 3–10 lines. E.g. "Our sales dropped 18% this quarter. The main drop was in the South region. Two competitors launched new products in Q3. Our team is blaming pricing."]

My role in this: [Are you analysing it? Presenting it? Being asked for your opinion?]
I need to present this to: [who — your boss, a client, the board, your team]

Do the following:
1. Identify the 3 most important insights hidden in this situation that most people would miss
2. Write a "So What?" statement — one paragraph that explains why this matters and what should be done
3. Give me 3 possible recommendations, ranked from safest to boldest
4. Anticipate the 2 hardest questions I'll be asked and give me sharp 2-line answers for each
5. Suggest how to open my presentation so the room pays attention from the first sentence
What You Get

Senior-level strategic analysis, sharp recommendations, and pre-answered tough questions — in minutes.

Use It When

Before any high-stakes meeting, when you need to present data, or when asked for your opinion on a complex issue.

Pro Tip

Use this before every monthly review meeting. Over 3 months, your reputation as a strategic thinker will be locked in — because you'll consistently have the sharpest takes in the room.

Try These Variations
Add "My manager tends to push back on bold recommendations — soften the language but keep the substance"
Ask it to format the output as a one-page memo you can share before the meeting
Feed it an industry article and ask: "What does this mean for my company specifically?"

The Personal Brand Builder

Become the known expert in your field — without spending hours writing.
Why This Prompt Matters

In an age of automation, visibility = security. The professional who is known — on LinkedIn, in their industry, in their company — will always have options. The invisible professional, no matter how talented, is the first to be cut.

This prompt turns your everyday work experiences into LinkedIn posts, articles, and thought leadership content that builds your reputation — consistently. 

I am a [job title] in the [industry] industry with [X years] of experience.

My audience on LinkedIn: [Who follows you or who you want to attract — recruiters, clients, peers, etc.]

Here is something that happened at work recently, or a lesson I learned, or an opinion I have:
[Write 2–5 lines describing your experience, observation, or belief. It doesn't need to be polished — raw is fine.]

Write 3 different LinkedIn posts based on this:
1. A short, punchy post (under 150 words) that hooks with a bold opening line
2. A storytelling post (200–300 words) that builds tension and ends with a takeaway
3. A "controversial opinion" style post that challenges a common belief in my industry

For each post: include a strong first line, the body, and a call-to-action question at the end.
Tone: confident, conversational, Indian professional — not corporate-speak.
What You Get

3 ready-to-post LinkedIn drafts in 3 different formats — just pick your favourite and hit publish.

Use It When

Once a week, every week. Consistency is the only personal branding strategy that works.

Pro Tip

Post once a week for 8 weeks in a row. You don't need viral posts — you need consistent presence. After 8 weeks, recruiters and clients will start reaching out to you, not the other way around.

Try These Variations
Ask it to write a carousel script (10 slides) instead of a text post for higher reach
Add "The post should subtly position me as someone who knows how to use AI at work"
Use this for Twitter/X threads too — same prompt, just ask for thread format

The Competitor Intelligence Report

Know what your competition is doing before they do it themselves.
Why This Prompt Matters

Large companies have entire market research teams. You have this prompt. In 10 minutes, you can build a competitor intelligence brief that would take a junior analyst two days — and it's better, because AI synthesises patterns across industries that humans miss.

Use this monthly to stay permanently one step ahead, even with a team of 3. 

My business: [Describe your business in 2–3 lines — what you sell, who you sell to, and where you operate]

My main competitors: [Name 2–4 competitors, or describe the type if you don't know names]

Do a competitor intelligence analysis:
1. What strategies are businesses like mine typically using to attract and retain customers right now?
2. What are the 3 biggest gaps most small businesses in my industry leave open — that I could exploit?
3. What is one thing the big players in my space do that I could copy cheaply with AI tools?
4. What customer complaints are common in my industry? (These are my opportunities)
5. Suggest 3 specific moves I could make in the next 30 days to differentiate myself from my competitors

Format as a short intelligence report I can share with my team.
What You Get

A full competitor brief with exploitable gaps, copyable tactics, and 30-day differentiation moves.

Use It When

Monthly planning, before launching a new product, or whenever a competitor makes a move you need to respond to.

Pro Tip

After the report, ask: "Which of these 3 moves would be hardest for my competitors to copy quickly, and why?" That's your sustainable advantage — and it's worth more than any tactic.

Try These Variations
Add your city/region to get hyper-local competitive intelligence: "I operate in Pune, targeting mid-income families"
Ask it to analyse a specific competitor's website and social strategy in detail
End with "What would a ₹10 crore company do in my position that I'm not doing yet?"

The Customer Pain Excavator

Find out what your customers secretly want — and say it back to them.
Why This Prompt Matters

The biggest reason small businesses fail at selling isn't the product — it's the messaging. They talk about features. Customers buy based on feelings and fears. This prompt bridges that gap by excavating the real psychological pain your customer feels and turning it into copy that converts.

Use this output to rewrite your Instagram bio, your website hero section, your DMs, and your sales pitches. 

My business: [What you sell and to whom — be specific]

My typical customer: [Age, occupation, lifestyle, and biggest frustration they come to you with]

What my product/service does: [Describe it in plain language]

Do the following:
1. Write a "Pain Stack" — the surface pain, the deeper pain, and the secret fear underneath (the one they won't say out loud)
2. Write 5 Instagram caption hooks that speak directly to the secret fear
3. Write a 3-sentence sales pitch that leads with pain, transitions to hope, and ends with my product as the obvious solution
4. Suggest 3 objections my customer has before buying — and give me the exact language to overcome each one
5. Write a one-line tagline for my business that makes my ideal customer say "that's exactly me"
What You Get

Pain-based messaging, 5 caption hooks, a converted sales pitch, and objection handlers — all in one run.

Use It When

Launching a product, refreshing your content strategy, or whenever you feel like you're posting but nobody's buying.

Pro Tip

Take the "secret fear" line AI generates and paste it word-for-word into your next Instagram post as the opening line. Watch your engagement double. Customers feel seen — and that's the only marketing that works.

Try These Variations
Run this for 3 different customer profiles if you serve multiple segments
Ask: "Now rewrite my current 'About' page using the Pain Stack framework"
Add "My customers have tried [X] before and it didn't work" to get even more targeted objection handling

The 30-Day Growth Sprint

Your complete content + sales plan for next month — built in one sitting.
Why This Prompt Matters

Most small business owners either post randomly or not at all — because planning takes too long. This prompt generates an entire month of content and sales activity in one go: what to post, when to push the sale, and what to say.

You'll spend 20 minutes running this prompt and walk away with a 30-day playbook that used to cost ₹20,000 from a marketing agency. 

My business: [What you sell, price range, and who you sell to]
My main platform: [Instagram / WhatsApp / LinkedIn / local market]
My goal for next month: [E.g. get 10 new paying customers / launch a new product / grow followers by 500]
My current content style: [Reels / static posts / stories / none yet]

Build my 30-day growth sprint:
1. Week-by-week content focus (Week 1 = awareness, Week 2 = education, etc.)
2. A content calendar with 3 posts per week — include: post type, topic, and one-line hook for each
3. 2 "sales moments" in the month where I push hard for purchases — give me the exact script for each
4. One lead magnet idea I could offer free to grow my list or following
5. One WhatsApp broadcast message I could send to existing customers to drive repeat purchase

Make it realistic for a solo business owner with 1–2 hours per day available.
What You Get

A full 30-day content + sales calendar with hooks, scripts, sales moments, and a lead magnet — ready to execute.

Use It When

The last week of every month, planning the next. Run it for 3 months in a row and your revenue will show it.

Pro Tip

After generating the calendar, pick just the 2 highest-impact posts and do those first. A done post beats a perfect plan. Once those 2 are live, come back and execute the rest.

Try These Variations
Add "There is a festival / sale season coming up: [name it]" for a seasonal twist on the plan
Ask it to write all 12 post captions in one go after generating the calendar
Add "My budget for paid ads this month is ₹[X]" to get a paid + organic hybrid plan
