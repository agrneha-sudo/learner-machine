Prompt 1 of 7

The "Brutally Honest
Career Advisor" Prompt

When you're stuck, confused, or afraid to take the next step in your career

Why This Changes Everything

Most people make career decisions based on what their parents want, what their friends are doing, or pure fear. Very few people ever get truly honest, personalized career advice.

This prompt makes ChatGPT act as that one mentor who tells you the truth — not what you want to hear, but what you need to hear. No flattery. No vague advice. Just clarity.

You are a brutally honest career advisor with 20 years of
experience. Do not sugarcoat anything.

Here is my situation:
- Current job/field: [your current work]
- Age: [your age]
- What I actually enjoy: [what you genuinely like doing]
- What I'm afraid of: [your real fear]
- My financial situation: [brief description]

Give me:
1. An honest assessment of where I'm headed if I stay on
   this path for 5 more years
2. The one thing I'm probably avoiding that I need to face
3. Three concrete, realistic next steps I can take this month
4. The biggest mistake people in my situation make

Be direct. I can handle the truth.
What You Get

A clear picture of where your current path leads — written by an "advisor" with no emotional investment, no bias, and no reason to spare your feelings.

Real Example

Rohan, 26, was in a job he hated. Used this prompt. ChatGPT showed him he was 3 years away from a senior role he'd still hate. He changed tracks within 2 months.

💡

Why it's life-changing

Clarity is the most valuable thing in career decisions. Most people spend years in the wrong direction not because they're lazy — but because no one ever told them the truth. This prompt fills that gap.

Prompt 2 of 7

The "Personal Finance
Doctor" Prompt

When money disappears every month and you don't know where it goes

Why This Changes Everything

A personal financial advisor costs ₹5,000–₹20,000 per session in India. Most people never get one. This prompt gives you a personalized money plan in under 2 minutes — for free.

The key is being completely honest in the prompt. The more specific your numbers, the more useful the advice.

You are a personal finance expert who specializes in helping
Indians aged 22–35 build wealth from scratch.

My financial situation:
- Monthly income: ₹[your income]
- Fixed expenses (rent, EMIs, bills): ₹[amount]
- Variable expenses (food, transport, entertainment): ₹[amount]
- Current savings: ₹[amount]
- Debt: [any loans or credit card debt]
- Financial goal: [what you want — house, travel, investment]
- Timeframe: [when you want to achieve it]

Give me:
1. An honest assessment of my current financial health
2. A practical monthly budget I can actually stick to
3. The one money habit that will change everything for me
4. How to reach my goal — with real numbers and a timeline
5. What I should do this week to start

Prompt 3 of 7

The "Difficult Conversation
Script" Prompt

When you need to say something hard — to your boss, family, friend, or partner

Why This Changes Everything

Most people avoid difficult conversations for months — sometimes years — because they don't know how to start or they're afraid of the reaction. Relationships suffer. Opportunities are missed. Resentment builds.

This prompt helps you structure the exact words to say. You walk in prepared, calm, and clear.

I need to have a difficult conversation and I need help
preparing for it.

The situation:
- Who I'm talking to: [boss / parent / partner / friend]
- The relationship: [how long, how important]
- What I need to say: [the actual thing you need to communicate]
- What I'm afraid will happen: [their reaction you're dreading]
- What outcome I want: [what success looks like]
- Their likely perspective: [how they probably see this situation]

Give me:
1. An opening line that isn't aggressive but is honest
2. A full conversation script — what to say, in what order
3. How to handle the 3 most likely negative reactions
4. What NOT to say (common mistakes in this type of conversation)
5. How to end the conversation well regardless of how it goes
Use Cases

Asking for a salary raise. Telling parents about a career change. Breaking up with someone. Confronting a friend. Quitting a job. Setting boundaries.

Real Example

Priya used this before asking her boss for a ₹1.5L raise. She prepared every objection in advance. The conversation took 12 minutes. She got the raise.

🗣️

Why it's life-changing

The conversations you avoid are the ones running your life. This prompt removes the fear of starting by giving you the exact words — so you can stop postponing and start resolving.

Prompt 4 of 7

The "Learn Anything in
48 Hours" Prompt

When you need to get good at something fast — a skill, subject, or concept

Why This Changes Everything

Most learning is inefficient. People spend weeks on courses and come out knowing 20% of what actually matters. The brain learns best with structure, context, and rapid repetition of core concepts.

This prompt forces AI to teach you the way the best teachers teach — with the 20% that gives you 80% of the results.

I want to learn [skill/subject] as quickly as possible.

My context:
- Why I need to learn this: [your specific reason]
- My current level: [complete beginner / some basics / intermediate]
- Time I can give: [hours per day] for [number of days]
- How I learn best: [reading / watching / doing / all three]

Create for me:
1. The 20% of concepts I must learn to get 80% of results
2. A day-by-day learning plan for my exact timeframe
3. The single most common mistake beginners make — and how to avoid it
4. 3 free resources (YouTube / websites) that are genuinely the best
5. A simple test I can give myself to know I've actually learned it
6. How a true expert thinks about this subject differently than a beginner

Prompt 5 of 7

The "Design My Perfect Day"
Prompt

When you feel busy all day but get nothing important done

Why This Changes Everything

Most people live reactively — responding to notifications, others' demands, and whatever feels urgent. They end each day exhausted but not satisfied. Productivity is not about doing more. It's about doing the right things at the right time.

This prompt builds your personal operating system — a daily structure designed around your biology, goals, and real life.

I want to design my ideal daily schedule to maximize
productivity and wellbeing.

My situation:
- Work/study hours: [when you work]
- Energy pattern: [morning person / night owl / afternoon peak]
- Top 3 goals right now: [list them]
- Biggest time wasters I know about: [social media / overthinking / etc]
- Must-do daily: [exercise / family time / prayer / etc]
- Sleep goal: [hours] from [time] to [time]

Design for me:
1. My ideal daily schedule — hour by hour from wake up to sleep
2. The non-negotiable "protected hours" I should block for deep work
3. A morning routine under 30 minutes that actually sets up a good day
4. An evening shutdown routine that helps me sleep better
5. One small habit to add this week that will have compound effects
6. What to do when the plan breaks (because it will)
The Problem It Solves

Feeling busy but unproductive. Wasting mornings. Scrolling social media for hours. Going to bed feeling guilty about what didn't get done.

Real Example

Amit, a freelancer, used this and realized he was doing his hardest work at his lowest energy time. One schedule shift = 3x more output in the same hours.

⚡

Why it's life-changing

Your day is your life in miniature. What you do every day is what you do with your life. A structured day built around your real energy and real goals — not someone else's template — changes everything over time.

Prompt 6 of 7

The "Personal Health
Coach" Prompt

When you want to get fit, eat better, or feel healthier but don't know where to start

Why This Changes Everything

A personal trainer in India costs ₹3,000–₹15,000 per month. A nutritionist costs similarly. Most people can't afford both. And generic advice from the internet doesn't match your body, schedule, or food preferences.

This prompt creates a personalized plan using your specific situation — not a template built for someone else.

Important: Always consult a real doctor for medical conditions. This is for general wellness, not medical advice.

You are a certified fitness and nutrition coach helping
an Indian person improve their health realistically.

My situation:
- Age & gender: [age and gender]
- Current fitness level: [never exercise / light activity / moderate]
- Main goal: [lose weight / gain muscle / more energy / better sleep]
- Time available for exercise: [minutes per day, which days]
- Equipment: [none / gym / home equipment]
- Food preferences: [vegetarian/non-veg, any restrictions]
- Biggest health challenge: [what you've tried and failed at]
- Budget for food/fitness: ₹[monthly]

Give me:
1. A realistic 4-week workout plan I can actually start tomorrow
2. A daily meal plan using common Indian foods available in my city
3. The one diet change that will have the biggest impact immediately
4. Why I've probably failed before — and what to do differently
5. How to track progress without becoming obsessed with the scale
💪

Why it's life-changing

Health affects everything — your energy, your mood, your confidence, your work. Most people know this but never start because "where to start" feels overwhelming. This prompt removes that blocker with a plan that fits your actual life.

Prompt 7 of 7

The "Big Decision
Clarity" Prompt

When you're at a crossroads and can't figure out what to do

Why This Changes Everything

Big decisions are paralyzing because our emotions cloud our thinking. We loop the same thoughts endlessly. We ask friends who tell us what we want to hear. We delay until the decision is made for us.

This prompt gives you a structured framework to see your decision clearly — considering what your mind, your future self, your values, and the real data actually say.

I'm facing a major decision and I need complete clarity.

The decision:
- Option A: [first option — describe fully]
- Option B: [second option — describe fully]
- (Optional) Option C: [if there's a third path]

My context:
- What matters most to me in life right now: [values/priorities]
- What I'm afraid of with each option: [fears]
- What I'm excited about with each option: [hopes]
- What my gut is saying: [instinct, even if it scares you]
- Who is affected by this decision: [people impacted]
- The deadline I'm facing: [when you must decide]

Analyze this and give me:
1. What the data and logic clearly says (ignoring emotion)
2. What my gut is actually trying to tell me
3. The question I'm not asking that I should be
4. What my 70-year-old self would tell me to do
5. The reversibility of each option — which one can I undo?
6. Your honest recommendation — pick one and tell me why
Works For

Job offer decisions. City relocation. Business vs job. Relationship decisions. Leaving education. Major purchases. Any choice you've been stuck on for weeks.

The Hidden Gem

"The question I'm not asking" — this one usually cuts through everything. It surfaces the real issue you've been avoiding. Users say this single output point alone was worth it.

🎯

Why it's life-changing

The quality of your life is determined by the quality of your decisions. Most people make major life decisions while emotionally flooded and informationally incomplete. This prompt fixes both — giving you structure when your mind is chaos.
